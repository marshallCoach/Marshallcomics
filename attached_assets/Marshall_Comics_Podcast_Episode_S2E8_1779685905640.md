**HOST:** Box fifty-eight. DC two thousand and one through two thousand and nine. The big one.

**JAMES:** Justice League of America. The full Brad Meltzer run from issue zero through issue twelve. Then Dwayne McDuffie takes over through issue thirty-four. That is the complete arc of one of DC's most significant team books of the two-thousands. Meltzer brought back the classic lineup. McDuffie brought in black heroes and Milestone characters and fought editorial interference the entire way. The complete run is in this collection.

**RICK:** Meltzer issue one is ten dollars raw. The complete run as a Whatnot package — the Meltzer era is why the team matters, the McDuffie era is why it was brave — forty to seventy dollars as a curated arc. The story of Justice League of America in the two-thousands is actually a story about how DC both elevated and constrained one of its best writers.

**MIKE:** And Final Crisis! Grant Morrison! COMPLETE! Seven issues! Darkseid WINS! The universe ENDS! And it is SITTING. IN. BOX FIFTY-EIGHT.

**JAMES:** Final Crisis is perhaps the most misunderstood major event in DC history. Morrison wrote it as a poem, not a plot. Darkseid literally falls on the universe and crushes it. The Anti-Life Equation is broadcast through the internet. Superman sings a miracle note. It is completely deranged in the best possible way, and the complete seven-issue run confirms the collection has been built by someone with genuine taste.

**RICK:** Issue one is twelve dollars raw. The complete seven-issue run as a curated package — with the DC Universe zero prelude included — is fifty to eighty dollars on Whatnot. Open with the Darkseid wins frame. Tell the story. The audience will follow.

**HOST:** Box fifty-nine. DC New Fifty-Two and Convergence. James, I'll give you this one.

**JAMES:** This is one of the most comprehensive DC collections I have seen in a single box. Wonder Woman by Brian Azzarello and Cliff Chiang, near-complete zero through fifty-two. The most significant feminist reimagining of Diana Prince in the history of the character. Greek mythology treated as genuinely horrific. The gods are not benevolent. They are hungry and petty and dangerous. Near-complete run.

**MIKE:** The Multiversity! Grant Morrison! COMPLETE! The main series PLUS all seven tie-ins! Pax Americana is a compressed masterwork! The Just is a sly satire of superhero excess! Ultra Comics is the comic that becomes self-aware and attacks the reader! ALL OF THEM! COMPLETE!

**JAMES:** The Multiversity is arguably the greatest piece of superhero meta-fiction ever published. Each issue is set on a different alternate Earth. Each issue is a different genre. Issue one is an adventure story. Pax Americana is a Watchmen deconstruction. Thunderworld Adventures is pure Silver Age joy. And they all connect into a grand unified theory of why superhero stories matter. Complete. In Box Fifty-Nine.

**RICK:** The Multiversity number one is twenty dollars raw. The complete set with all tie-ins — nine issues total — is a ninety to one-twenty dollar Whatnot package. The pitch is simple: Grant Morrison mapped every possible version of DC Comics and published it as a comic. Nothing like it exists.

**MIKE:** And then. CONVERGENCE.

**JAMES:** Twenty series. Each a two-part story. Forty issues. Plus the main eight-issue Convergence series. Roberto confirmed he has both parts of every two-parter. We've catalogued all of it.

**MIKE:** Gail Simone writes Convergence: Nightwing and Oracle. Nightwing and Oracle. Dick and Barbara. Together. Before Flashpoint took them away. And Roberto has both parts. I need a moment.

**RICK:** Convergence as a financial proposition is interesting. The individual issues are five to eight dollars each. But as a complete event — the main series plus all forty tie-in issues, forty-eight comics total — it is the most complete Convergence collection I have ever seen in private hands. Heritage candidate if someone wants the full story. Whatnot if you want the audience engagement of revealing one two-parter per show.

**HOST:** Box sixty. Inhumans, Eternals, and Captain Marvel.

**JAMES:** The Eternals section of this box contains two complete landmark runs side by side. Neil Gaiman and John Romita Junior, two thousand and six, seven issues. And Kieron Gillen and Esad Ribic, two thousand and twenty-one, twelve issues. These two runs represent the alpha and omega of modern Eternals storytelling. Gaiman made them human. Gillen made them tragic.

**RICK:** Gaiman Eternals number one is fifteen dollars raw. The complete seven-issue set is sixty to eighty dollars. Gillen Eternals number one is twelve dollars. The complete twelve-issue run is seventy to ninety. Having both complete is genuinely rare.

**MIKE:** KARNAK! Warren Ellis! One issue! The Inhuman who can find the flaw in ALL THINGS! Ellis gave him a DOJO and a PHILOSOPHY and it is MAGNIFICENT and it is in BOX SIXTY!

**JAMES:** The Captain Marvel section is the most comprehensive Captain Marvel run I have seen catalogued. Kelly Sue DeConnick volumes seven and eight, essentially complete. Margaret Stohl's Mighty Captain Marvel. Kelly Thompson's volume ten, near-complete through issue fifty. And Alyssa Wong's current volume twelve run. This is Carol Danvers from the moment she took the Captain Marvel name through to the present day.

**RICK:** Captain Marvel by Kelly Thompson issue one is twelve dollars. The near-complete Thompson run — issues one through fifty, most of them present — is a hundred and twenty to a hundred and eighty dollars as a package. This is one of the most complete modern Captain Marvel collections in existence.

**MIKE:** And CM PUNK wrote DRAX. CM PUNK. The wrestler. Wrote a Marvel comic. Eleven issues. COMPLETE. In Box Sixty-Two. This is the most unexpected complete run in the collection and I love it unconditionally.

**HOST:** Box sixty-one. Fantastic Four. James, you were very quiet when I told you what was in it.

**JAMES:** I needed a moment. The Fantastic Four collection in box sixty-one is extraordinary. Mark Waid's run, including issue five hundred — the landmark anniversary issue with the Doom slash Doom slash Doom cover. Jonathan Hickman's run, which is one of the greatest sustained superhero narratives of the twenty-first century. Matt Fraction's run, which is deeply underrated. Dan Slott's run, near-complete. Ryan North's current run, the most charming FF in a decade, including issue seven — Legacy seven hundred — the landmark anniversary numbering.

**RICK:** Fantastic Four number five hundred — Waid, Wieringo — is fifteen dollars raw. Hickman issue five-seventy-one, where the Council of Reeds is introduced — eight dollars raw. Dan Slott's relaunch issue one — twelve dollars. Ryan North's issue one — twelve dollars. And Legacy seven hundred — the Ryan North issue seven — twenty dollars. The collection spans from five hundred to the present day with minimal gaps. As a legacy run Whatnot package, this is three to four shows minimum of material.

**MIKE:** The Clobbering Time series is in there. The twelve-in-time series. The New Fantastic Four — Peter David! Hulk and Ghost Rider and Spider-Man and Wolverine AS the Fantastic Four, from twenty-twenty-two! COMPLETE! Peter David wrote that and it is COMPLETE and it is in BOX SIXTY-ONE.

**JAMES:** Marvel Knights Fantastic Four by Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa and Steve McNiven is also worth noting. Issues two through twenty-seven, near-complete. A dark, mature, horror-adjacent FF run that is critically underappreciated and financially overlooked. McNiven went on to draw Civil War immediately after. This is where he developed the cinematic style that made that series legendary.

**HOST:** Box sixty-two. Annihilation, Nova, Silver Surfer, Guardians.

**JAMES:** Annihilation: Nova, four issues, complete. Annihilation: Silver Surfer, four issues, complete. Dan Abnett and Andy Lanning's Nova volume four, with the Rising from Annihilation launch issue. This box is the foundation of Marvel's cosmic renaissance.

**RICK:** Annihilation: Nova number one is eight dollars. The complete Nova Rising from Annihilation run — which launched the landmark Abnett-Lanning cosmic era — is a forty to sixty dollar package. **MIKE:** SILVER SURFER: GHOST LIGHT. John Jennings. Valentine De Landro. An Afrofuturist reimagining of Silver Surfer. Five issues. Complete. This book almost no one knows about. And it is in Box Sixty-Two. Hidden. Waiting. And now it is found.

**JAMES:** Silver Surfer Ghost Light is genuinely one of the most visually distinctive superhero comics published in the last five years. Jennings and De Landro bring a Yoruba-influenced cosmic aesthetic to the Silver Surfer that is unlike anything else Marvel has published. Five issues. Complete.

**MIKE:** And OLD MAN QUILL. Ethan Sacks. Twelve issues. COMPLETE. Future Star-Lord in a post-apocalyptic galaxy. It is Old Man Logan but in space and it has no right to be as good as it is and it is COMPLETE.
